How this score is weighted

These weights are fixed and apply to every product, so the score is a consistent, comparable judgement — not something you can dial to a flattering number.

Why these weights?

Nutritional Quality — 50%. A food's nutrient profile (sugar, salt, saturated fat, fibre) has the most direct and best-established link to long-term health, so it carries the most weight.

Additives — 30%. Additives reflect a precautionary read of the current evidence. Most approved additives are low-risk, so they matter — but less than the core nutrient content.

Processing Level — 20%. The NOVA classification captures ultra-processing, which is associated with poorer health outcomes. The link is real but less direct than nutrient content, so it carries the least weight.

A separate cap can override the weighted total when a product is nutritionally poor or ultra-processed, so a clean additive profile alone can't earn a high score.
